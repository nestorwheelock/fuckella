## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A **foreign national tenant** who does not speak Spanish signed a notarized lease contract (escritura ante Notario Publico) in Cancun, Quintana Roo. The contract was:

- Written **entirely in Spanish** with no translation provided
- **Rushed** through at the notary's office with no interpreter present
- Signed before the tenant could understand its terms
- The **tenant paid the notary fee**, yet the **landlord chose the notary** (conflict of interest)
- The tenant **never received a copy** of the signed contract

The landlord attempted a bait-and-switch rent increase ($12,500 agreed by text, then "$13,500" at signing). The tenant refused to sign and requested time to have an attorney review the contract. The landlord was caught off guard by the pushback and insisted "nothing changed from last year." The tenant told the landlord they were stressed about leaving for the US and didn't want to address complaints in that moment — particularly since many complaints were about the landlord's lack of follow-through and embarrassing her in front of the notary would not help the relationship. The tenant believes the relationship can be saved and is paying **$12,000** (the rate before the disputed increase) given the bait-and-switch and unresolved issues.

**CRITICAL TIMELINE:** The current lease expires **March 11, 2026**. The tenant paid March rent on **March 1** at $12,000 — 10 days before expiration. The landlord **accepted this payment without objection** at the old rate. This acceptance of rent at the original rate, before the contract even expires, is devastating for the landlord's position. When March 11 passes and the tenant continues in possession without opposition, **tacita reconduccion** (Art. 2739) activates automatically on March 12, converting the lease to an **indefinite term at the rate being paid ($12,000)**. Meanwhile:

- The landlord made **written promises via WhatsApp** (AC replacement, furniture removal) and broke them
- The tenant has performed **extensive repairs** that were legally the landlord's responsibility
- The landlord continues **accepting rent at the original rate**
- The HVAC replacement took **3 months** — in tropical Caribbean heat

**The law is overwhelmingly on the tenant's side.** The Quintana Roo Civil Code provides:

1. **Art. 383(d):** The manifestation of will **does not exist** when a person does not know the language of the contract — potentially rendering the entire contract void (absolute nullity)
2. **Art. 193-205:** Consent obtained through error, fraud, or deliberate concealment (reticencia) is invalid
3. **Art. 2739:** The lease has automatically renewed for an **indefinite term** (tacita reconduccion) at the **original rent**
4. **Art. 2687:** The landlord's maintenance obligations are **mandatory by law** and cannot be contracted away
5. **Art. 2698:** Tenant protection provisions are **non-waivable** ("sera nula toda estipulacion en contrario")
6. **Art. 87, 98, 220:** The notary bears **professional liability** for failing to verify comprehension

The tenant has multiple defenses against eviction and strong counterclaims for reimbursement, damages, and potential nullity of abusive contract terms.

## 3. APPLICABLE LAWS — VERBATIM ARTICLE TEXT

All citations below are **verbatim** from the Codigo Civil para el Estado de Quintana Roo (2023 edition).

### 3.1 Consent and Will (Vicios de la Voluntad)

**Articulo 193.** La voluntad no es valida si ha sido dada por error, por miedo o por dolo.

*English: Consent is not valid if it has been given by error, by fear, or by fraud.*

**Articulo 194.** El error sobre la naturaleza del negocio juridico lo anula, si creyendo una de las partes que ha celebrado determinado contrato, la otra parte cree que el celebrado es otro negocio distinto.

*English: An error regarding the nature of the juridical act renders it void, if one of the parties believed they entered into a certain contract while the other party believes the contract entered into is a different transaction altogether.*

**Articulo 198.** Se entiende por dolo en los negocios juridicos, cualquiera maquinacion o artificio que se emplee para inducir a error o mantener en el a alguno de los que lo celebran y por mala fe, la disimulacion del error de uno de estos una vez conocido.

*English: Fraud in juridical transactions is understood as any machination or artifice employed to induce error or to keep one of the parties in error, and bad faith is understood as the concealment of the error of one of the parties once it becomes known.*

**Articulo 203.** La ignorancia o ausencia de conocimientos respecto al asunto materia del negocio, es causa de nulidad de este, cuando induce a error a quien la padece.

*English: Ignorance or lack of knowledge regarding the subject matter of the transaction is a cause of nullity when it induces error in the person who suffers from it.*

**Articulo 204.** Tambien es causa de nulidad la reticencia si induce a error.

*English: Reticence is also a cause of nullity if it induces error.*

**Articulo 205.** La reticencia es el silencio voluntariamente guardado por una de las partes, acerca de un hecho o circunstancia que la otra tendria interes en conocer para estar en aptitud consciente de celebrar el negocio.

*English: Reticence is the silence voluntarily maintained by one of the parties regarding a fact or circumstance that the other party would have an interest in knowing in order to be in a conscious position to enter into the transaction.*

**Articulo 211.** No es licito renunciar para lo futuro la nulidad que resulte del dolo, de la mala fe, reticencia o de la violencia.

*English: It is not lawful to waive in advance the nullity that results from fraud, bad faith, reticence, or duress.*

### 3.2 Manifestation of Will — Language Requirement

**Articulo 383.** La manifestacion de la voluntad no existe si quien la emite es:

a) Un nino menor de siete anos de edad;

b) Una persona privada de inteligencia por locura, idiotez o imbecilidad, aunque tenga intervalos lucidos, salvo en este ultimo caso, disposicion de la ley;

c) Un sordomudo que no sepa leer ni escribir, ni darse a entender mediante interprete por el lenguaje escrito o mimico en que se imparte instruccion escolar a los de su clase;

**d) Las personas que no sepan leer ni escribir, ni conozcan el idioma en que pudiera aparecer haber contratado.**

*English: The manifestation of will does not exist if the person who makes it is: a) A child under seven years of age; b) A person deprived of intelligence by insanity, idiocy, or imbecility, even if they have lucid intervals, except in the latter case by provision of law; c) A deaf-mute who does not know how to read or write, nor make themselves understood through an interpreter by the written or sign language in which school instruction is given to those of their kind; d) Persons who do not know how to read or write, or who do not know the language in which they may appear to have contracted.*

### 3.3 Nullity Classification

**Articulo 384.** La falta de cualquiera de los requisitos que menciona el articulo 135 produce la nulidad absoluta del acto, la que operaria de pleno derecho si no hay un principio o inicio de ejecucion.

*English: The absence of any of the requirements mentioned in Article 135 produces the absolute nullity of the act, which would operate as a matter of law if there has been no beginning or commencement of performance.*

**Articulo 385.** Si hay un principio de ejecucion, la nulidad absoluta debe ser necesariamente declarada en juicio.

*English: If there has been a beginning of performance, the absolute nullity must necessarily be declared in a judicial proceeding.*

**Articulo 386.** La ilicitud en el objeto, en el motivo o fin o en la condicion del negocio juridico, produce la nulidad absoluta de este; pero tal nulidad es relativa cuando por disposicion de una ley no reune todos los caracteres enumerados en el articulo 388.

*English: Illegality in the object, motive or purpose, or condition of the juridical act produces its absolute nullity; however, such nullity is relative when, by provision of law, it does not meet all the characteristics enumerated in Article 388.*

**Articulo 387.** La nulidad relativa del negocio juridico tambien se produce: I.- Por incapacidad legal del autor o de una de las partes, siempre que no se trate de las personas enumeradas en el articulo 383. II.- Por vicios de la voluntad; III.- Porque esta no se haya manifestado en la forma que la ley establece; y IV.- Por lesion.

*English: The relative nullity of a juridical act is also produced: I. By legal incapacity of the author or of one of the parties, provided it does not involve the persons enumerated in Article 383; II. By defects of consent; III. Because consent was not manifested in the form established by law; and IV. By lesion.*

**Articulo 388.** La nulidad absoluta puede ser invocada por quien tenga interes ilegitimo en hacerla valer; es imprescriptible; no se extingue por renuncia expresa o tacita ni por confirmacion o ratificacion, y los tribunales, aun de oficio, pueden tomarla en consideracion.

*English: Absolute nullity may be invoked by anyone who has a legitimate interest in asserting it; it is imprescriptible; it is not extinguished by express or tacit waiver nor by confirmation or ratification, and the courts, even on their own motion, may take it into consideration.*

**Articulo 390.** La nulidad absoluta se produce de pleno derecho y por el solo hecho de que se cometa la infraccion.

*English: Absolute nullity is produced as a matter of law and by the mere fact that the violation is committed.*

**Articulo 394.** La nulidad por vicios de la voluntad, lesion o incapacidad que no sea absoluta, solo puede invocarse por el que ha sufrido esos vicios, se ha perjudicado por la lesion o es el incapaz.

*English: Nullity due to defects of consent, lesion, or incapacity that is not absolute may only be invoked by the person who has suffered those defects, has been harmed by the lesion, or is the incapacitated person.*

**Articulo 398.** La accion de nulidad fundada en error prescribe en el lapso de dos anos, a no ser que el que incurrio en el lo conozca antes de que expire ese termino, pues en este caso la accion prescribe a los noventa dias contados desde aquel en que el error fue conocido.

*English: The action for nullity based on error prescribes within a period of two years, unless the person who committed the error discovers it before that term expires, in which case the action prescribes within ninety days counted from the date on which the error was discovered.*

**Articulo 401.** El negocio juridico viciado de nulidad solo en parte, no es totalmente nulo si sus partes integrantes pueden legalmente subsistir separadas, a menos que se demuestre que al celebrarse el negocio se quiso que solo integramente subsistiera.

*English: A juridical act vitiated by nullity only in part is not totally void if its integral parts can legally subsist separately, unless it is demonstrated that at the time of entering into the transaction the parties intended that it only subsist as a whole.*

### 3.4 Notary Duties

**Articulo 220.** Siempre que por requerirlo la ley o por voluntad de los interesados, un documento privado sea ratificado ante Notario, Registrador, Juez de Primera Instancia, Menor, de Paz o autoridad administrativa, estos, en sus respectivos casos, deben cerciorarse de la identidad de las partes, de su capacidad y de la autenticidad de las firmas.

*English: Whenever, as required by law or by the will of the interested parties, a private document is ratified before a Notary, Registrar, Judge of First Instance, Minor Court Judge, Justice of the Peace, or administrative authority, these officials, in their respective cases, must verify the identity of the parties, their capacity, and the authenticity of the signatures.*

### 3.5 Civil Liability

**Articulo 87.** Todo hecho del hombre, que no constituya delito, ejecutado por culpa o negligencia, sean estas simples o dolosas, que causen dano a otro en su persona o en sus bienes, obliga a su autor a la reparacion del dano y a la indemnizacion de los perjuicios de acuerdo con las disposiciones de este Codigo.

*English: Every act of a person that does not constitute a crime, carried out through fault or negligence, whether simple or willful, that causes harm to another in their person or property, obliges its author to repair the damage and to indemnify the losses in accordance with the provisions of this Code.*

**Articulo 98.** Las personas que ejercen profesiones tecnicas o liberales, estan obligadas a responder de los danos y perjuicios causados en el desempeno de su encargo, por sus auxiliares, ayudantes, colaboradores, pasantes, enfermos o empleados.

*English: Persons who practice technical or liberal professions are obligated to be liable for damages and losses caused in the performance of their duties by their assistants, helpers, collaborators, interns, trainees, or employees.*

**Articulo 104.** El propietario de un edificio es responsable de los danos que resulten de la ruina de todo o parte de el, si esta sobreviene por falta de reparaciones necesarias o por vicios de construccion.

*English: The owner of a building is liable for damages resulting from the ruin of all or part of it, if such ruin occurs due to lack of necessary repairs or construction defects.*

### 3.6 Public Order and Weak Party Protection

**Articulo 8.** La voluntad de los particulares no puede eximir de la observancia de la ley, ni alterarla o modificarla. Solo pueden renunciarse los derechos privados que no afecten directamente al interes publico, cuando la renuncia no perjudique derechos de tercero.

*English: The will of private parties cannot exempt from observance of the law, nor alter or modify it. Only private rights that do not directly affect the public interest may be waived, when the waiver does not prejudice the rights of third parties.*

**Articulo 13.** Los actos juridicos, ejecutados contra el tenor de las leyes prohibitivas o del orden publico, seran nulos, excepto en los casos en que la ley ordene lo contrario.

*English: Juridical acts executed contrary to the tenor of prohibitive laws or public order shall be void, except in cases where the law orders otherwise.*

**Articulo 21.** En caso de conflicto de derechos, a falta de ley expresa que sea aplicable, la controversia se decidira a favor del que trata de evitarse perjuicios y no a favor del que pretenda obtener lucro. Si el conflicto fuere entre derechos iguales o de la misma especie, se decidira observando la mayor igualdad posible entre los interesados, y a favor de la parte mas debil.

*English: In case of a conflict of rights, in the absence of an express applicable law, the dispute shall be decided in favor of the party seeking to avoid harm and not in favor of the party seeking to obtain profit. If the conflict is between equal rights or rights of the same kind, it shall be decided by observing the greatest possible equality between the interested parties, and in favor of the weaker party.*

**Articulo 23.** La proteccion del orden publico incluira la proteccion del economica, social o culturalmente debil.

*English: The protection of public order shall include the protection of the economically, socially, or culturally weak.*

**Articulo 24.** Sera nulo el acto juridico en que alguien, explotando la suma ignorancia, notoria inexperiencia o extrema miseria de otro, obtiene un lucro excesivo que sea evidentemente desproporcionado.

*English: A juridical act shall be void in which someone, exploiting the extreme ignorance, notorious inexperience, or extreme poverty of another, obtains an excessive profit that is evidently disproportionate.*

### 3.7 Lease — Landlord Obligations

**Articulo 2682.** El arrendamiento debe otorgarse por escrito y su vigencia se inicia, salvo convenio en contrario, con la entrega del bien.

*English: The lease must be granted in writing and its validity begins, unless otherwise agreed, with the delivery of the property.*

**Articulo 2687.** El arrendador esta obligado, aunque no haya pacto expreso:

I.- A mantener el bien arrendado en estado de servir para el uso convenido, y a falta de convenio al respecto, para el uso a que aquel, por su misma naturaleza, estuviere destinado, haciendo para ello todas las reparaciones necesarias;

II.- A no estorbar ni embarazar de manera alguna el uso del bien arrendado, o del suministro de los servicios basicos, a no ser por causa de reparaciones urgentes e indispensables.

III.- A garantizar el uso o goce pacifico del bien por todo el tiempo del contrato; y

IV.- A responder de los danos y perjuicios que sufra el arrendatario por los defectos o vicios ocultos del bien, anteriores al arrendamiento.

*English: The landlord is obligated, even without an express agreement: I. To maintain the leased property in a condition fit for the agreed-upon use, and in the absence of an agreement on this point, for the use to which the property is naturally destined, making all necessary repairs for this purpose; II. Not to obstruct or hinder in any way the use of the leased property or the supply of basic services, except for the purpose of urgent and indispensable repairs; III. To guarantee the peaceful use and enjoyment of the property for the entire duration of the contract; and IV. To be liable for damages and losses suffered by the tenant due to defects or hidden vices of the property existing prior to the lease.*

**Articulo 2688.** El arrendador no puede, durante el arrendamiento, mudar la forma del bien arrendado, ni intervenir en el uso legitimo de el, salvo el caso designado en la fraccion II del articulo anterior, a cuyo efecto el arrendatario debera poner en conocimiento del arrendador, a la brevedad posible, la necesidad de las reparaciones, bajo pena de pagar les danos y perjuicios que su omision cause.

Si el arrendador no cumpliere con hacer las reparaciones necesarias para el uso a que este destinado el bien, quedara a eleccion del arrendatario demandar la rescision del arrendamiento u ocurrir al juez para que estreche al arrendador al cumplimiento de su obligacion, mediante el procedimiento rapido que se establezca en el Codigo de Procedimientos Civiles; en la inteligencia de que si el arrendador no hiciere las reparaciones en el termino que en ejecucion de sentencia fije el juez, este podra autorizar al arrendatario a que las haga a cuenta de rentas.

El juez, segun las circunstancias del caso, decidira sobre el pago de los danos y perjuicios que se causen al arrendatario por falta de oportunidad en las reparaciones.

*English: The landlord may not, during the lease, change the form of the leased property, nor intervene in its legitimate use, except in the case designated in Section II of the preceding article, for which purpose the tenant must notify the landlord as soon as possible of the need for repairs, under penalty of paying damages and losses caused by such omission. If the landlord fails to make the necessary repairs for the use to which the property is destined, the tenant may choose either to demand rescission of the lease or to petition the court to compel the landlord to fulfill their obligation through the expedited procedure established in the Code of Civil Procedure; with the understanding that if the landlord does not make the repairs within the period fixed by the court in the execution of judgment, the court may authorize the tenant to make them and deduct the cost from rent. The court, according to the circumstances of the case, shall decide on the payment of damages and losses caused to the tenant by the lack of timeliness in the repairs.*

**Articulo 2692.** El arrendador responde de los vicios o defectos del bien arrendado que impidan su uso, aunque el no los hubiese conocido o hubiesen sobrevenido sin culpa del arrendatario, en el curso del arrendamiento. El arrendatario puede pedir en este caso la disminucion de la renta o la rescision del contrato, salvo que se pruebe que tuvo conocimiento antes, o en el momento de celebrarlo, de los vicios o defectos del bien arrendado.

*English: The landlord is liable for vices or defects of the leased property that prevent its use, even if the landlord did not know of them or they arose without fault of the tenant during the course of the lease. The tenant may in this case request a reduction of the rent or rescission of the contract, unless it is proven that the tenant had knowledge of the vices or defects of the leased property before or at the time of entering into the contract.*

**Articulo 2694.** Corresponde al arrendador pagar las mejoras hechas por el arrendatario:

I.- Si en el contrato, o posteriormente, lo autorizo para hacerlas y se obligo a pagarlas;

II.- Si se trata de mejoras utiles y por culpa del arrendador se rescindiese el contrato;

III.- Cuando el contrato fuere por tiempo indeterminado, si el arrendador autorizo al arrendatario para que hiciera mejoras y antes de que transcurra el tiempo necesario para que el arrendatario quede compensado con el uso de las mejoras de los gastos que hizo, da el arrendador por concluido el arrendamiento.

Las mejoras a que se refieren las fracciones II y III deberan ser pagadas por el arrendador, no obstante que en el contrato se hubiese estipulado que las mejoras quedasen a beneficio del bien arrendado.

*English: It is the landlord's obligation to pay for improvements made by the tenant: I. If in the contract, or subsequently, the landlord authorized the tenant to make them and undertook to pay for them; II. If the improvements are useful and the contract is rescinded due to the landlord's fault; III. When the contract is for an indefinite period, if the landlord authorized the tenant to make improvements and before enough time has passed for the tenant to be compensated through the use of the improvements for the expenses incurred, the landlord terminates the lease. The improvements referred to in Sections II and III must be paid by the landlord, notwithstanding that the contract may have stipulated that the improvements would remain for the benefit of the leased property.*

*English: The tenant is obligated: V. To make repairs for minor deterioration suffered by the property or its accessories that are regularly caused by inhabiting a house.*

### 3.9 Habitability and Non-Waivable Provisions

**Articulo 2698.** Si por caso fortuito o fuerza mayor se impide totalmente al arrendatario el uso del bien arrendado, no se causara renta mientras dure el impedimento, y si este dura mas de dos meses, podra el arrendatario pedir la rescision del contrato. En cambio, si por las mismas causas solo se impide en parte el uso del bien, podra el arrendatario pedir la reduccion parcial de la renta, a juicio de peritos, a no ser que las partes opten por la rescision del contrato, si el impedimento dura el tiempo fijado en el parrafo anterior. **Lo dispuesto en este articulo no es renunciable.**

*English: If by fortuitous event or force majeure the tenant is totally prevented from using the leased property, no rent shall accrue while the impediment lasts, and if it lasts more than two months, the tenant may request rescission of the contract. Conversely, if the same causes only partially prevent the use of the property, the tenant may request a partial reduction of the rent, as determined by experts, unless the parties opt for rescission of the contract if the impediment lasts for the period specified in the preceding paragraph. The provisions of this article are not waivable.*

**Articulo 2707.** El arrendatario que por causa de reparaciones pierda el uso total del bien, tiene derecho a no pagar el precio del arrendamiento y a pedir la rescision del contrato, en tanto que si la perdida del uso no es total sino parcial, solo podra pedir la reduccion equitativa de la renta; pero tambien podra demandar la rescision, si dicha perdida parcial del uso dura mas de dos meses.

*English: The tenant who loses total use of the property due to repairs has the right not to pay the lease price and to request rescission of the contract; whereas if the loss of use is not total but partial, the tenant may only request an equitable reduction of the rent; however, the tenant may also demand rescission if such partial loss of use lasts more than two months.*

**Articulo 2708.** En los arrendamientos que han durado mas de cinco anos y cuando el arrendatario ha hecho mejoras de importancia en la finca arrendada, tiene este, si esta al corriente en el pago de la renta, derecho a que, en igualdad de condiciones, se le prefiera a otro interesado en el nuevo arrendamiento de la finca. Tambien gozara del derecho del tanto si el propietario quiere vender la finca arrendada, aplicandose en lo conducente lo dispuesto en el articulo 1999.

*English: In leases that have lasted more than five years and when the tenant has made significant improvements to the leased property, the tenant, if current on rent payments, has the right, under equal conditions, to be preferred over any other interested party in the new lease of the property. The tenant shall also enjoy the right of first refusal if the owner wishes to sell the leased property, applying as appropriate the provisions of Article 1999.*

**Articulo 2709.** No podra darse en arrendamiento una localidad que no reuna las condiciones de higiene y salubridad exigidas por el Codigo Sanitario. El arrendador que no haga las obras que ordenen las autoridades del ramo como necesarias para que tina localidad sea habitable e higienica, es responsable de los danos y perjuicios que los inquilinos sufran por esa causa. No puede renunciarse anticipadamente el derecho de cobrar esta indemnizacion.

*English: A premises that does not meet the hygiene and health conditions required by the Health Code may not be leased. The landlord who does not carry out the works ordered by the relevant authorities as necessary for a premises to be habitable and hygienic is liable for the damages and losses suffered by the tenants for that reason. The right to collect this indemnification may not be waived in advance.*

### 3.10 Lease Renewal and Termination

**Articulo 2714.** Todos los arrendamientos de predios rusticos o urbanos que no se hayan celebrado por tiempo expresamente determinado, concluiran a voluntad de cualquiera de las partes contratantes, previo aviso a la otra parte dado en forma indubitable con dos meses de anticipacion si el predio es urbano, y con un ano si es rustico.

*English: All leases of rural or urban properties that have not been entered into for an expressly determined period shall conclude at the will of either of the contracting parties, with prior notice given to the other party in an indubitable manner with two months' advance notice if the property is urban, and one year if it is rural.*

**Articulo 2738.** A mas tardar quince dias despues de vencido un contrato de arrendamiento, tendra derecho el inquilino, siempre que este al corriente en el pago de las rentas, a pedir que se le prorrogue hasta por un ano ese contrato. Podra el arrendador aumentar hasta un diez por ciento, la renta anterior, siempre que demuestre que los alquileres en la zona de que se trate, han sufrido alzas que justifiquen el aumento.

Quedan exceptuados de la obligacion de prorrogar el contrato de arrendamiento los propietarios que quieran habitar la casa, ocupar personalmente el local destinado a despacho, industria o comercio, o cultivar la finca cuyo arrendamiento ha vencido, bajo el concepto de que si pasados tres meses de la ejecutoriacion del fallo respectivo el arrendador no habitare la casa, no cultivare el predio o no ocupare personalmente el local destinado a despacho, comercio o industria, sera condenado, en el procedimiento sumarisimo que senale el Codigo de la materia, a entregarle al inquilino, por via de indemnizacion, el importe de la renta correspondiente a los meses que fije discrecionalmente el Juez o Tribunal y que no podra exceder de doce meses.

*English: No later than fifteen days after the expiration of a lease contract, the tenant shall have the right, provided they are current on rent payments, to request that the contract be extended for up to one year. The landlord may increase the previous rent by up to ten percent, provided the landlord demonstrates that rents in the relevant area have experienced increases that justify the raise. Exempt from the obligation to extend the lease contract are owners who wish to inhabit the house, personally occupy the premises used for an office, industry, or commerce, or cultivate the property whose lease has expired, with the understanding that if three months after the final judgment the landlord has not inhabited the house, cultivated the property, or personally occupied the premises used for an office, commerce, or industry, the landlord shall be ordered, through the summary procedure specified by the applicable Code, to pay the tenant, by way of indemnification, the amount of rent corresponding to the number of months set at the discretion of the Judge or Court, which may not exceed twelve months.*

**Articulo 2739.** Si despues de terminado el arrendamiento y su prorroga, si la hubo, continua el arrendatario sin oposicion en el goce y uso del predio, y este es rustico, se entendera renovado el contrato por otro ano, y por tiempo indefinido si la finca es urbana. En cualquiera de ambos casos el arrendatario debera pagar la renta que corresponda al tiempo que exceda al del contrato, con arreglo a lo que pagaba.

*English: If after the termination of the lease and its extension, if any, the tenant continues without opposition in the enjoyment and use of the property, and the property is rural, the contract shall be understood as renewed for another year, and for an indefinite period if the property is urban. In either case, the tenant must pay the rent corresponding to the time exceeding that of the contract, in accordance with what was previously paid.*

**Articulo 2740.** Cuando haya prorroga en el contrato de arrendamiento o tacita reconduccion a que respectivamente se refieren los dos articulos anteriores, cesan las obligaciones otorgadas por un tercero para la seguridad del arrendamiento, salvo convenio en contrario.

*English: When there is an extension of the lease contract or tacit renewal as respectively referred to in the two preceding articles, the obligations granted by a third party for the security of the lease shall cease, unless otherwise agreed.*

**Articulo 2741.** El arrendador puede exigir la rescision del contrato:

I.- Por falta de pago de la renta en los terminos prevenidos en la fraccion I del articulo 2683;

II.- Por usarse el bien en contravencion a lo dispuesto en la fraccion III del articulo 2695; y

III.- Por el subarriendo del bien en contravencion a lo dispuesto en el articulo 2716.

*English: The landlord may demand rescission of the contract: I. For failure to pay rent in the terms prescribed in Section I of Article 2683; II. For using the property in violation of the provisions of Section III of Article 2695; and III. For subletting the property in violation of the provisions of Article 2716.*

**Articulo 2744.** Si el predio dado en arrendamiento fuere enajenado judicialmente, el contrato de arrendamiento subsistira, a menos que aparezca que se celebro dentro de los sesenta dias anteriores al secuestro de la finca.

*English: If the leased property is judicially sold, the lease contract shall subsist, unless it appears that it was entered into within the sixty days prior to the seizure of the property.*

**Articulo 44.** El que sin mandato y sin estar obligado a ello se encargue de un asunto de otro, debe obrar conforme a los intereses del dueno del negocio.

## 4. LEGAL ANALYSIS

### 4.1 Defective Consent — Language Barrier and Foreign National Status

**This is the most powerful argument in the entire case.**

#### 4.1.1 The Nuclear Option: Art. 383(d) — Will Does Not Exist

> **Art. 383(d):** "Las personas que no sepan leer ni escribir, **ni conozcan el idioma en que pudiera aparecer haber contratado.**"
>
> Translation: Persons who cannot read or write, **or who do not know the language in which they appear to have contracted.**

The law states that for such persons, **"la manifestacion de la voluntad no existe"** — the manifestation of will **does not exist**. This is not merely a defect; it means the will was **never formed**.

**Application to this case:**
- The tenant is a foreign national who does not speak Spanish
- The contract was written entirely in Spanish
- No translation was provided
- No interpreter was present
- The tenant therefore **did not know the language in which they appear to have contracted**

Under Art. 383(d), the tenant's manifestation of will **does not exist**. Combined with Art. 384, the absence of a required element (consent/will per Art. 135) produces **absolute nullity**.

**Art. 388 consequences of absolute nullity:**
- Can be invoked by anyone with legitimate interest
- Is **imprescriptible** (never expires)
- Cannot be extinguished by express or tacit waiver
- Cannot be cured by confirmation or ratification
- Courts may consider it **sua sponte** (even without being asked)

**Strategic consideration:** Art. 401 provides that a partially void contract can have its valid parts survive. The tenant may not want to void the ENTIRE contract (since the lease itself benefits the tenant). Instead, the tenant can argue that specific abusive clauses (like "tenant responsible for all repairs") are absolutely null while the beneficial lease terms survive. This is a surgical strike: void the bad clauses, keep the lease.

#### 4.1.2 Vicios de la Voluntad (Defects of Consent)

Even if a court does not apply Art. 383(d) directly, multiple consent defects exist:

**Error (Art. 193, 203):** The tenant's ignorance of Spanish means they were in fundamental error about the contract terms. Art. 203 specifically states that "ignorancia o ausencia de conocimientos respecto al asunto materia del negocio" (ignorance about the subject matter) is a cause of nullity when it induces error.

**Reticencia (Art. 204-205):** The landlord's failure to disclose — or ensure the tenant understood — critical contract terms (especially the "all repairs" clause) constitutes **reticencia**: "el silencio voluntariamente guardado por una de las partes, acerca de un hecho o circunstancia que la otra tendria interes en conocer." The tenant obviously had an interest in knowing that the contract supposedly made them responsible for all repairs.

**Dolo (Art. 198):** Rushing a non-Spanish speaker through a Spanish-only notarized signing without translation or interpreter can constitute "maquinacion o artificio que se emplee para inducir a error o mantener en el a alguno de los que lo celebran."

**Non-waivable (Art. 211):** "No es licito renunciar para lo futuro la nulidad que resulte del dolo, de la mala fe, reticencia o de la violencia." Any clause in the contract that purports to waive these nullity rights is itself void.

#### 4.1.3 Exploitation of Ignorance (Art. 24)

> "Sera nulo el acto juridico en que alguien, **explotando la suma ignorancia, notoria inexperiencia** o extrema miseria de otro, obtiene un lucro excesivo que sea evidentemente desproporcionado."

A foreign national who doesn't speak Spanish, signing a contract they can't read, which contains terms making them responsible for all repairs in a property they don't own — this is textbook exploitation of "suma ignorancia" and "notoria inexperiencia."

#### 4.1.4 Statute of Limitations for Nullity Actions

- **Absolute nullity (Art. 383(d) + 388):** IMPRESCRIPTIBLE. No time limit. Ever.
- **Error-based nullity (Art. 398):** 2 years from the contract, OR 90 days from when the error was discovered (whichever is later)
- **Form-based nullity (Art. 393):** 2 years from celebration of the contract

### 4.2 Notary's Failure of Duty

#### 4.2.1 The Notary's Legal Obligation

> **Art. 220:** Officials who ratify documents must "cerciorarse de la identidad de las partes, de su capacidad y de la autenticidad de las firmas."

"Cerciorarse" means "to make certain of" or "to verify." The notary has an affirmative duty to verify not just identity and authenticity of signatures, but **capacity** (capacidad). A person who does not understand the language of the document they are signing lacks the practical capacity to consent to its terms.

#### 4.2.2 The Notary's Breach

The notary failed to:
1. **Verify the tenant's comprehension** of the Spanish-language document
2. **Require or provide a translator/interpreter** for a visibly foreign, non-Spanish-speaking party
3. **Ensure informed consent** — the very purpose of notarization
4. **Protect the interests of the paying client** — the tenant paid the notary fee

#### 4.2.3 Conflict of Interest

The notary situation presents a clear conflict:
- The **tenant paid the notary fee**, establishing the tenant as the notary's client
- The **landlord chose the notary**, creating a relationship of trust/loyalty between landlord and notary
- The notary's duty was owed to **both parties**, but particularly to the paying party
- The notary failed to protect the paying client's interests while facilitating a contract that benefited the party who selected the notary

#### 4.2.4 Professional Liability

> **Art. 87:** "Todo hecho del hombre, que no constituya delito, ejecutado por culpa o negligencia, sean estas simples o dolosas, que causen dano a otro en su persona o en sus bienes, obliga a su autor a la reparacion del dano y a la indemnizacion de los perjuicios."

> **Art. 98:** "Las personas que ejercen profesiones tecnicas o liberales, estan obligadas a responder de los danos y perjuicios causados en el desempeno de su encargo."

The notary's negligent failure to ensure the tenant's comprehension caused direct damage: the tenant signed a contract containing terms (especially the "all repairs" clause) they never understood, leading to thousands of pesos in repair costs the tenant should never have borne.

**Available actions:**
- Complaint to the **Colegio de Notarios de Quintana Roo**
- Civil liability claim under Arts. 87 and 98
- Request to the notary for a **certified copy** of the escritura (the tenant has an absolute right to this as a party to the instrument)

### 4.3 Failure to Deliver Contract Copy

> **Art. 2682:** "El arrendamiento debe otorgarse por escrito..."

The landlord never provided the tenant with a copy of the signed contract. Since the contract was notarized, a certified copy (testimonio) exists in the notary's protocol. The tenant can request it directly from the notary. However, the landlord's failure to provide a copy compounds the consent problem — the tenant cannot comply with terms they neither understood at signing nor have been able to review since.

**Key principle (Art. 21):** Ambiguity is resolved "a favor de la parte mas debil" (in favor of the weaker party). The tenant — a foreigner without a contract copy, without Spanish language ability — is unquestionably the weaker party.

### 4.4 Tacita Reconduccion — Automatic Lease Renewal

> **Art. 2739:** "Si despues de terminado el arrendamiento y su prorroga, si la hubo, continua el arrendatario sin oposicion en el goce y uso del predio [...] y por tiempo indefinido si la finca es urbana."

**The lease WILL automatically renew for an INDEFINITE TERM on March 12, 2026 because:**

1. **Puerto Morelos / Colonia Villas 1 is urban** ("finca urbana") — it is NOT rural
2. The current lease expires **March 11, 2026**
3. The tenant will continue occupying after the term ends
4. The landlord has **not opposed** continued occupancy
5. The landlord **accepted March rent at $12,000** on March 1 — before the contract even expired — without objection
6. By accepting payment at $12,000 before expiration, the landlord has implicitly acquiesced to continued occupancy at the old rate

**The March 1 payment is the linchpin:**
- The landlord accepted rent for a period that spans beyond the contract expiration date
- She accepted $12,000, not $12,500 (the text-agreed amount) and not $13,500 (the bait-and-switch amount)
- She made no objection or reservation when accepting the payment
- This payment acceptance, combined with the tenant's continued possession after March 11, satisfies ALL elements of Art. 2739

**Timeline:**
| Date | Event | Legal Effect |
|------|-------|-------------|
| March 1, 2026 | Tenant pays $12,000 rent | Landlord accepts at old rate — no objection |
| March 4, 2026 | Today — tenant refused bait-and-switch contract | No new contract signed |
| March 11, 2026 | Contract expires | Let it pass quietly |
| March 12, 2026 | **Tacita reconducción activates** | Lease becomes indefinite at $12,000/month |
| April 1, 2026 | Tenant pays $12,000 again | Further locks rate if accepted |
| Every month after | Same payment, same acceptance | Rate increasingly cemented |

**Consequences once activated (March 12+):**

- **Rent is locked at $12,000:** "con arreglo a lo que pagaba" — the tenant pays according to what they were already paying. The landlord's acceptance of $12,000 on March 1 establishes this as "lo que pagaba."
- **Cannot be terminated without 2 months written notice** (Art. 2714) — verbal statements don't count, and the 15-day contract clause is void
- **The landlord CANNOT retroactively claim the contract "expired"** — once tacita reconduccion activates, the contract is renewed by operation of law
- **Third-party guarantees (fianzas) terminated** (Art. 2740) — the landlord lost their security
- **Art. 2738 right:** Even before tacita reconducción, the tenant has 15 days after expiration to demand a 1-year extension with a maximum 10% rent increase (which the landlord must prove is market-justified)

**Even if the landlord gives proper 2-month notice**, the tenant has the right to demand reimbursement for all repairs and improvements before vacating (Art. 2694-III, applicable to indefinite-term leases).

**STRATEGIC NOTE:** Between now and March 11, do absolutely nothing confrontational. Let the date pass quietly. The best outcome is that March 11 comes and goes like any other day. The clock is working entirely for the tenant.

### 4.5 AC Agreement — Written WhatsApp Promise

The landlord's AC/HVAC replacement promise was made **in writing via WhatsApp**. This is significant because:

1. **WhatsApp messages are admissible evidence** in Mexican courts
2. The promise constitutes a **written commitment** — stronger than a mere verbal agreement
3. The landlord eventually replaced the AC, **confirming the obligation existed**, but took **3 months** — during which the tenant suffered in Cancun's tropical heat (average temperatures 30-35C)

**Legal basis for the AC claim:**

- **Art. 2687-I:** Landlord must maintain the property "en estado de servir para el uso convenido" — AC is necessary for habitability in Cancun
- **Art. 2709:** Property must meet health and hygiene standards — a dwelling without AC in tropical Cancun fails this standard
- **Art. 2698:** During the 3 months without AC, the tenant was partially impeded from full use of the property, entitling them to **partial rent reduction** — and this right is **non-waivable** ("lo dispuesto en este articulo no es renunciable")
- **Art. 2707:** If loss of use exceeded 2 months (it did — 3 months), the tenant can demand rescission OR equitable rent reduction

**Furniture removal promise:** The landlord also promised via WhatsApp to remove unwanted furniture but has not done so. Under Art. 2688, the landlord "no puede mudar la forma del bien arrendado, ni intervenir en el uso legitimo." Conversely, leaving unwanted items that interfere with the tenant's use violates the duty of peaceful enjoyment (Art. 2687-III).

### 4.6 Nullity of "Tenant Responsible for All Repairs" Clause

The contract allegedly contains a clause making the tenant responsible for **all** repairs. This clause is **null and void** on multiple independent grounds:

#### Ground 1: Consent Never Existed (Art. 383(d))
The tenant did not know Spanish. Their will regarding this clause **does not exist**. The clause has no legal force.

#### Ground 2: Mandatory Law Cannot Be Waived (Art. 8, 2687)
Art. 2687 uses the phrase "esta obligado, **aunque no haya pacto expreso**" — the landlord is obligated "even without express agreement." This is mandatory law that cannot be contracted around.

#### Ground 3: Public Order Violation (Art. 13, 23)
Acts contrary to prohibitive laws or public order are null (Art. 13). Tenant protection is explicitly part of public order (Art. 23 — protection of the "economica, social o culturalmente debil").

#### Ground 4: Explicit Non-Waivability (Art. 2698, 2709)
Art. 2698 ends with "lo dispuesto en este articulo no es renunciable." Art. 2709 states landlord liability for habitability failures "no puede renunciarse anticipadamente." These provisions demonstrate that tenant-protection lease articles are non-waivable public-order norms.

#### Ground 5: Exploitation of Ignorance (Art. 24)
Inserting an "all repairs" clause in a contract signed by a non-Spanish speaker who couldn't understand it constitutes exploitation of "suma ignorancia."

#### Ground 6: Partial Nullity (Art. 401)
Even if the contract is not entirely void, the "all repairs" clause can be severed and voided independently while the rest of the contract survives.

### 4.12 Protection Against Retaliatory Eviction

If the tenant exercises legal rights (SAT report, PROFECO complaint, notary complaint, habitability inspection) and the landlord subsequently attempts eviction, the following protections apply:

**Abuse of Rights (Art. 32):** Any abuse of rights by private parties is a cause of civil liability. A landlord who files eviction proceedings specifically because the tenant exercised a legal right is abusing the eviction process itself. That is actionable.

**Civil Liability (Art. 87):** Every act executed with fault or negligence that causes damage obligates the author to repair the damage and compensate for losses. A retaliatory eviction filing that causes documented harm — moving costs, distress, legal fees — creates liability.

**Actos Propios Doctrine:** If the landlord accepted rent peacefully for months, then suddenly files eviction immediately after a SAT report or PROFECO complaint, the **timing becomes devastating evidence**. A judge will see the sequence: legal complaint filed, then eviction filed days later. This pattern argues strongly that the eviction was pretextual — motivated by retaliation, not legitimate grounds.

**Weak Party Protection (Art. 21):** When there is a conflict of rights, it is decided in favor of the party seeking to avoid harm rather than the party seeking profit. Filing a SAT report or PROFECO complaint is exercising a legal right. Retaliatory eviction would be protecting illegal income. Art. 21 clearly favors the tenant.

**Illegal Eviction:** Any informal eviction attempt — cutting utilities, changing locks, harassment — constitutes an independent legal claim entirely separate from the retaliation argument, with serious consequences including civil fines, sanctions, damages claims, and potential restitution of the property.

### 4.15 Notary Liability Under the Ley del Notariado para el Estado de Quintana Roo

#### 4.15.1 A Mexican Notary Is Not a US Notary

A critical distinction for this case: a Mexican *Notario Publico* is **not** equivalent to a US notary public. In the US, a notary simply verifies identity and witnesses signatures. In Mexico, a notary is a **licensed public official with active legal duties** including:

- **Verifying the legality** of every clause in documents they certify
- **Refusing to notarize** contracts containing illegal provisions
- **Ensuring all parties have full understanding** and informed consent
- **Certifying that the document complies** with applicable law
- **Protecting all parties** — not just the party who hired them

Mexican notaries have professional legal training equivalent to an attorney and are expected to exercise that expertise actively. They are gatekeepers of legality.

#### 4.15.2 The Notary's Obligation to Verify Legality of Contract Terms

Under the **Ley del Notariado para el Estado de Quintana Roo**, notaries are expressly required to refuse to authorize acts that are contrary to law or public order. This is not discretionary — it is a mandatory professional obligation.

When the notary certified the lease contract containing a clause making the tenant responsible for all maintenance — a clause that directly contradicts **Art. 2687** of the Codigo Civil (mandatory landlord maintenance obligations) — the notary had a **legal obligation to refuse to certify that clause** or, at minimum, to flag it explicitly and explain its legal implications to both parties.

The notary did neither. They certified an illegal clause as valid.

#### 4.15.3 The Notary's Specific Failures in This Case

The notary's breaches are cumulative and severe:

#### 4.15.4 Governing Regulatory Framework

Mexican notaries are regulated by three overlapping authorities:

2. **Colegio de Notarios de Quintana Roo** — the professional disciplinary body that investigates complaints and can impose sanctions including suspension or revocation of the notary's *patente* (license)
3. **Codigo Civil de Quintana Roo** — specifically Arts. 87, 98, and 220 on professional liability, and Arts. 383(d), 384, 388 on nullity for lack of informed consent

The notary law explicitly requires notaries to refuse to authorize acts that are contrary to law or public order. ### 4.16 Holdover Provisions and Double Rent Penalty — Null and Void

#### 4.16.1 The Clause in Question

The landlord's contracts contain a "Conventional Penalty" clause (Clause 13 / *Decima Tercera*) imposing **double rent** (2x the monthly rent) as a penalty. The 2026 bait-and-switch contract added a new holdover paragraph:

> *"Ahora bien, si la Arrendadora tuviere que promover la desocupacion por haber vencido el Contrato, los Arrendatarios pagaran la Pena Convencional por cada mes de ocupacion posterior al vencimiento, independientemente de la Contraprestacion que se continue devengando **sin que el Contrato se considere renovado.**"*
>
> Translation: "Furthermore, if the Landlord must pursue eviction proceedings due to the Contract having expired, the Tenants shall pay the Contractual Penalty for each month of occupancy after expiration, regardless of the Consideration that continues to accrue, **without the Contract being considered renewed.**"

This clause attempts three things simultaneously:
1. Impose **double rent** ($27,000/month) for staying after contract expiration
2. Extract this penalty **on top of** regular rent (not instead of it)
3. **Explicitly defeat tacita reconduccion** by declaring the contract "not renewed"

All three are legally indefensible.

#### 4.16.2 Tacita Reconduccion Eliminates the Holdover Period Entirely

**Art. 2739** provides that if the tenant continues in possession after the lease expires and the landlord does not oppose, the lease renews automatically for an indefinite term under the same conditions.

This is a **statutory right** — it operates by force of law, not by contractual agreement. The key legal principles:

1. **Tacita reconduccion is triggered by operation of law** (Art. 2739), not by the contract. A contractual clause cannot override a statutory provision.

2. **Once tacita reconduccion activates, there IS no "holdover period."** The tenant is not "holding over" — they are occupying under a legally renewed lease. The concept of holdover ceases to exist because the lease continues.

3. **The landlord's contract expires March 11, 2026. Tacita reconduccion activates March 12, 2026.** From that date forward, the tenant occupies under a valid, indefinite-term lease at $12,000/month. There is no gap, no holdover, no expiration to trigger the penalty.

4. **The clause "sin que el Contrato se considere renovado" directly contradicts Art. 2739.** The Civil Code says the contract IS renewed by operation of law. The landlord's clause says it is NOT renewed. The statute wins. Always.

#### 4.16.3 Punitive Holdover Clauses Contradict Statutory Tenant Rights — Null Under Art. 13

> **Art. 13:** "Los actos ejecutados contra el tenor de las leyes prohibitivas o de interes publico seran nulos de pleno derecho."
>
> Translation: Acts executed contrary to prohibitive laws or laws of public interest shall be null by operation of law.

The holdover penalty clause is null for multiple independent reasons:

**Reason 1: Contradicts Art. 2739 (Tacita Reconduccion)**
Art. 2739 is a tenant protection provision of public order. A contractual clause that attempts to negate a statutory tenant right by imposing punitive consequences for exercising that right is contrary to a law of public interest and therefore null under Art. 13.

**Reason 2: Contradicts Art. 2709 (Non-Waivable Rights)**
> **Art. 2709:** "Cualquier estipulacion que imponga al arrendatario obligaciones mas onerosas que las expresamente senaladas en este Codigo, sera nula."
>
> Translation: Any stipulation that imposes on the tenant obligations more onerous than those expressly indicated in this Code shall be null.

The Code does not impose a double-rent penalty for remaining in possession after lease expiration — it provides for automatic renewal. A clause imposing 2x rent as a holdover penalty is "more onerous" than the Code requires and is therefore **null by express statutory command**.

**Reason 3: Contradicts Art. 2738 (Rent Increase Limits)**
Art. 2738 caps rent increases at 10% annually and requires the landlord to prove market justification. A clause that doubles the rent overnight from $13,500 to $27,000 (or from $12,000 to $24,000) circumvents the statutory cap on rent increases. It is a disguised rent increase of 100%, far exceeding the 10% legal maximum.

**Reason 4: The Clause is Unconscionable (Art. 24)**
> **Art. 24:** Acts exploiting extreme ignorance or inexperience to obtain excessive and disproportionate profit are null.

A penalty that doubles the rent — extractable monthly, on top of regular rent, creating a potential liability of $27,000+ per month — is "evidentemente desproporcionado" (evidently disproportionate), especially when imposed on a tenant who demonstrably never understood the clause.

#### 4.16.4 A Non-Spanish Speaker Cannot Be Bound by Penalty Clauses They Never Knew Existed

This is the strongest argument of all, and it layers perfectly with the language barrier analysis from Section 4.1:

**Layer 1: The will never existed (Art. 383(d))**
A person who does not know the language of the contract has no manifestation of will. If the will does not exist, the penalty clause has no binding force. This is absolute nullity — imprescriptible, non-waivable, and incurable.

**Layer 2: The contract was never provided to the tenant**
The landlord never gave the tenant a copy of the signed contract. The tenant has never had the opportunity to read the penalty clause even with the aid of a translator. You cannot enforce a penalty against someone who has never been given the document containing it.

**Layer 3: The clause was added without disclosure (Year 2)**
The holdover penalty paragraph was **not present** in the Year 1 (2022) contract. It was added in the Year 2 (2023) contract — the same contract where the landlord told the tenants "only the price changed." The tenants were actively deceived about the existence of this clause.

**Layer 4: No attorney present when the clause was introduced**
Year 1 was signed with the tenant's attorney present. Year 2 — when the holdover penalty was added — was signed **without an attorney**. The landlord waited until the attorney was absent to introduce the punitive clause, while falsely assuring the tenants that "nothing changed except the price."

**Layer 5: The signing was rushed**
The Year 2 contract was sent the morning of the signing (3/6/23 at 9:26 AM), with the appointment that same afternoon. Even a Spanish speaker would not have had adequate time for meaningful review. For a non-Spanish speaker without an attorney, review was impossible.

**Combined effect:** The penalty clause fails on every level — statutory invalidity (Arts. 13, 2709, 2738, 2739), consent defect (Art. 383(d)), fraud in formation (Art. 198 — dolo), and unconscionability (Art. 24). It is unenforceable under any theory.

## 8. TENANT LOOPHOLES AND LANDLORD GOTCHAS

### LOOPHOLE 1: Art. 383(d) — The Language Bomb (Imprescriptible)
The tenant can invoke absolute nullity of any clause at **any time** — there is no statute of limitations. This means even years from now, the tenant can challenge the "all repairs" clause. The landlord can never cure this defect without a properly translated, properly consented new agreement.

### LOOPHOLE 2: Rent Prescription — The 2-Year Clock (Art. 2464)
If the landlord claims back rent or rent differences from more than 2 years ago, that claim is **prescribed**. Hard deadline, no exceptions.

### LOOPHOLE 3: Landlord Cannot Change Property During Lease (Art. 2688)
The landlord cannot alter the property form or interfere with legitimate use. If the landlord tries to "renovate you out" or start construction to make it uninhabitable — that's a violation.

### LOOPHOLE 4: Strict Liability for Defects (Art. 2692)
The landlord is liable for **all defects** — even ones they didn't know about and ones that appeared during the lease. This is strict liability. The hot water heater breaking again, the toilet, the shower drain — the landlord is liable regardless of knowledge.

### LOOPHOLE 5: Force Majeure Rent Reduction is NON-WAIVABLE (Art. 2698)
If circumstances partially prevent use of the property, the tenant can demand partial rent reduction. The contract CANNOT waive this. The phrase "lo dispuesto en este articulo no es renunciable" is absolute.

### LOOPHOLE 6: Total Loss of Use = No Rent (Art. 2707)
If repairs make the property wholly unusable, rent is suspended. If partial loss of use exceeds 2 months, the tenant can demand rescission.

### LOOPHOLE 7: Fianza Trap (Art. 2740)
When tacita reconduccion activated, any third-party guarantees automatically terminated. The landlord lost their security.

### LOOPHOLE 8: Personal Use Exception Has Teeth (Art. 2738)
If the landlord claims "personal use" to evict and doesn't actually move in within 3 months, a judge can order the landlord to pay **up to 12 months' rent** as damages.

### LOOPHOLE 9: Property Sale Doesn't Kill the Lease (Art. 2744)
If the property is sold, the lease survives. A new owner inherits the tenant.

### LOOPHOLE 10: Property Owner Liable for Building Damage (Art. 104)
The **property owner** is responsible for damages from "falta de reparaciones necesarias o por vicios de construccion." This is independent of the lease relationship — it's property law.

### LOOPHOLE 11: Gestion de Negocios (Arts. 44, 48)
The tenant managed the landlord's property by making repairs. This creates an independent legal basis for reimbursement, separate from the lease. Even if the lease is somehow found to support the landlord's position, gestion de negocios provides a backup claim.

### LOOPHOLE 12: Art. 2694 Final Paragraph — Improvements Trump Contract
Art. 2694 ends with: "Las mejoras a que se refieren las fracciones II y III deberan ser pagadas por el arrendador, **no obstante que en el contrato se hubiese estipulado que las mejoras quedasen a beneficio del bien arrendado.**" Even if the contract says improvements belong to the landlord, the landlord STILL has to pay when the lease terminates by their fault or on an indefinite-term lease.

### LOOPHOLE 13: The 15-Day Clause is Void — You Get 2 Months
The lease says 15 days to vacate. The law says 2 months for urban property (Art. 2714). The law wins. This clause is void under Arts. 8 and 13. The landlord cannot enforce a 15-day eviction timeline.

### LOOPHOLE 14: Property Ownership Challenge
If the landlord doesn't actually own the property (she said it's her parents'), demand proof of authorization. A landlord who can't prove authority to lease is in a very weak position.

### LOOPHOLE 15: Pre-Expiration Rent Acceptance — Rate Lock
The landlord accepted $12,000 rent on March 1, 2026 — 10 days before the contract expires on March 11. She accepted the OLD rate (not $12,500 per text agreement, not $13,500 per bait-and-switch), without objection or reservation, for a period spanning beyond the contract term. This establishes $12,000 as "lo que pagaba" under Art. 2739. Every subsequent month she accepts $12,000 further cements this rate. She cannot retroactively claim a different rate was agreed.

### LOOPHOLE 16: Rent Increase Requires PROOF — She Never Had Any (Art. 2738)
Art. 2738 caps rent increases at 10% per year AND requires the landlord to "demostrar" (prove with evidence) that rents in the specific zone have increased enough to justify the raise. She needs a commercial appraisal, comparable listings, or expert testimony — not just a WhatsApp message with a new number. She has never provided a single piece of evidence in 5 years. Every unjustified increase is legally challengeable.

### LOOPHOLE 17: 5-Year Tenancy = Right of First Refusal on Lease AND Sale (Art. 2708)
After 5+ years with significant improvements, the tenant has priority over any new tenant on equal terms AND the right of first refusal if the property is sold. The landlord cannot simply replace you with someone else. If her parents sell the house, they must offer it to you first at the same price.

### LOOPHOLE 18: Consignacion de Pago — Nuclear Defense Against Non-Payment Claims
If the landlord starts refusing rent to manufacture non-payment grounds (Art. 2741-I), the tenant can judicially deposit rent with the court. This proves willingness and ability to pay, completely neutralizing any non-payment claim.

### LOOPHOLE 19: Double Rent Holdover Penalty is Null — Four Independent Grounds (Arts. 13, 2709, 2738, 2739)
The landlord's contract imposes double rent as a "conventional penalty" for staying after expiration, and the 2026 bait-and-switch version explicitly states the contract shall NOT be considered renewed. This clause is null on four independent grounds: (1) it contradicts Art. 2739 (tacita reconduccion operates by law — contract cannot override it), (2) it violates Art. 2709 (any clause more onerous than the Code is null), (3) it circumvents Art. 2738 (100% rent increase disguised as a penalty exceeds the 10% cap), and (4) it is unconscionable under Art. 24 (evidently disproportionate). Furthermore, the holdover paragraph was secretly added in Year 2 while the landlord told the tenant "only the price changed" — the tenant never knew it existed. ## 10. STRATEGIC PRESSURE TOOLS

The following are legitimate legal tools available to tenants in Mexico. They are listed in order of what landlords typically fear most.

### 10.1 SAT Report (Tax Authority)
If the landlord is collecting rent without issuing **CFDI receipts** (comprobantes fiscales digitales), she is violating Mexican tax law. A report to the SAT about unreported rental income is completely legal, costs nothing, and creates enormous pressure. Landlords collecting informal payments without declaring income are extremely vulnerable.

### 10.2 Juicio de Amparo
The **amparo** is a constitutional remedy that can **halt eviction proceedings entirely** while the case is reviewed. It is not expensive to file and can freeze the situation for months or years. An **amparo preventivo** can be filed before eviction is even initiated if there is credible reason to believe it is imminent. This removes the landlord's timeline leverage completely.

### 10.3 PROFECO Complaint
Filing a formal complaint with the **Procuraduria Federal del Consumidor** creates a public record of the dispute and triggers a conciliation process. Multiple complaints from different tenants (if other tenants exist in the building) multiply the pressure dramatically.

### 10.4 Notary Complaint (Colegio de Notarios)
Filing a formal complaint with the **Colegio de Notarios de Quintana Roo** against the notary (No. 67, Luis Palacios, Cancun) costs nothing and creates significant pressure. The notary committed **six independent professional violations** (see Section 4.15.3): certifying an illegal maintenance clause that contradicts Art. 2687, proceeding without ensuring comprehension by a non-Spanish speaker, failing to require interpretation, rushing the signing, failing to deliver copies, and operating under a conflict of interest. Under the **Ley del Notariado para el Estado de Quintana Roo**, the notary was obligated to refuse to authorize these acts. A notary facing a complaint has strong incentive to acknowledge procedural failures rather than defend them — that acknowledgment becomes powerful evidence. This also forces the landlord's documentation ally to become a potential liability.

### 10.5 Municipal Habitability Inspection (Denuncia)
Filing a **denuncia** with Puerto Morelos' municipal authorities citing habitability violations (broken water heater, non-functional toilet, drainage issues) triggers an official inspection. If the property fails, the landlord faces fines and an official repair order — completely independent of the civil dispute.

### 10.6 Impugnacion Notarial (Nuclear Option)
Formally challenging the notarial act itself through the Colegio de Notarios AND civil court simultaneously. Given that a non-Spanish speaker was rushed through a signing with no interpreter and never received a copy, this is a **legitimate challenge** that could render the entire contract voidable. The landlord then has no document to stand on.

### 10.7 Demanding the Full Expediente Notarial
In court, the landlord must produce the original instrument. Demanding the full **expediente notarial** forces all irregularities in the notarial process into the open — and given your situation, there are likely many.

### 10.8 Utility Transfer Strategy
If utilities are in the landlord's name, she can theoretically use non-payment or service cuts as pressure. Transferring CFE (electricity) and CAPA (water) to your name removes this leverage. **However:** get your contract copy from the notary first to check if the lease restricts this. The process for **cambio de titular** at CFE and CAPA requires only valid ID, proof of address, and the current account number.

### 10.9 What the Landlord Fears Most (In Order)
1. SAT investigation into undeclared rental income
2. Amparo filing that freezes the timeline
3. PROFECO complaint creating a public record
4. Notary complaint threatening the notary's license
