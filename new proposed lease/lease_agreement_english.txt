LEASE AGREEMENT (the "CONTRACT") entered into by, on one part, MRS. ELLA RUTH JASSO PALACIO (the "LANDLORD"); and on the other part, MESSRS. NESTOR ELDON WHEELOCK and MARIO ALBERTO REYES CHAVEZ (the "TENANTS"), all acting in their own right and personal capacity, who agree to the following declarations and clauses:

DECLARATIONS

FIRST.- The Landlord declares, in her own right and personal capacity and under oath of truthfulness, that:

a) She is a natural person, of Mexican nationality, with full legal capacity to bind herself under the terms of this Contract.

b) She is the legitimate owner with full possession and use of the property described as: "House B", lot sixteen, block forty-one, located on Trasatlántico Street, in the "Villas Morelos" subdivision, superblock eighteen, Puerto Morelos, Municipality of Benito Juárez, State of Quintana Roo. Said property was registered under number 162086 (one, six, two, zero, eight, six) in the Public Registry of Property and Commerce, Cancún delegation.

c) For purposes of this contract, this property shall hereinafter be referred to as the "PROPERTY".

d) She wishes to grant occupancy and lease of the Property.

e) The Property is in good condition for use and is not subject to any judicial, labor, tax, or administrative proceedings.

SECOND.- The Tenants declare, in their own right and personal capacity and under oath of truthfulness, that:

a) They are natural persons; NESTOR ELDON WHEELOCK of American nationality, who proves his legal stay in the country with his valid immigration document and passport, which are attached to this contract as "Annex A"; and MARIO ALBERTO REYES CHAVEZ of Mexican nationality; both with full legal capacity to bind themselves under the terms of this Contract.

b) That in accordance with their interests, they wish to occupy the Property under lease.

c) That they have the financial capacity and sufficient solvency to fulfill their obligations, especially those of an economic nature, pursuant to this Contract.

d) That they have inspected the interior of the "PROPERTY", noting that the furnishings inside are in normal working condition.

Having stated the foregoing, the parties agree to the following:

CLAUSES

FIRST.- PURPOSE AND TERM. Under this Contract, the Landlord grants in favor of the Tenants the temporary use and enjoyment of the Property, with everything pertaining thereto by fact and by law, for a term of one year beginning on the thirteenth of March of two thousand twenty-six, and which shall therefore expire on the thirteenth of March of two thousand twenty-seven, the final date on which the Landlord shall receive from the Tenants the vacancy of the Property without the need for any legal proceedings or formalities.

SECOND.- RENT. For the occupancy referred to in the preceding clause, the Tenants agree to pay the Landlord a monthly rent of $13,500.00 (thirteen thousand five hundred pesos 00/100, Mexican National Currency).

These rents shall be paid by interbank transfer to the account of Mrs. Ella Ruth Jasso Palacio; Inbursa bank, account number: 50043459042 (five, zero, zero, four, three, four, five, nine, zero, four, two), interbank routing number: 036691500434590425 (zero, three, six, six, nine, one, five, zero, zero, four, three, four, five, nine, zero, four, two, five), on the eleventh of each month.

The Tenants shall guarantee payment of rent by signing twelve promissory notes; and in the event of early return of possession of the property, the Landlord shall return the original promissory notes corresponding to the months that were not occupied or accrued.

THIRD.- LATE INTEREST. Failure to make timely payment of rent within the range of days established in the preceding clause shall result in the payment of late interest charged to the Tenants and in favor of the Landlord, which shall accrue on the outstanding unpaid amounts and shall be calculated at an interest rate of 10% (ten percent) per month.

FOURTH.- DEPOSIT. The Tenants have previously delivered to the Landlord the amount of $13,500.00 (thirteen thousand five hundred pesos 00/100, Mexican National Currency) as a deposit to guarantee the repair of damages that the Tenants may cause to the Property and the payment of each and every public utility for which they are obligated to pay under this Contract.

The Deposit shall be refunded to the Tenants once the fulfillment of each and every one of their payment obligations has been verified, as well as the vacancy of the Property in good condition of use and with services in perfect working order; or alternatively, the resulting difference shall be refunded after offsetting payment of any unfulfilled obligations. The deposit shall not count as rent under any circumstances.

FIFTH.- USE OF THE PROPERTY. The Tenants shall use the Property exclusively as a residential dwelling, and may not give it any other use or commercial purpose without the written authorization of the Landlord.

The Landlord agrees to deliver the Property to the Tenants in adequate conditions for immediate use and in a condition suitable for the agreed-upon purpose, with the installations, furniture, accessories, and equipment detailed in the inventory duly signed by the parties and attached to this contract as "Annex B". The parties also specifically agree that any repair, maintenance, or upkeep that the furniture or equipment listed in Annex B may require shall be at the expense of the Tenants, who agree to return them in optimal conditions of use.

In the event that the Tenants use the Property for a purpose other than that established in the immediately preceding paragraph, the Landlord is entitled to rescind this contract without the need for a judicial declaration.

Page 4

SIXTH.- RECEPTION AND REMODELING WORK. By the mere act of occupying the Property, it is understood that the Tenants receive it in perfect working condition; therefore, upon return of the same, they must deliver it to the Landlord, committing to be responsible for any damages caused to the Property through their fault, carelessness, negligence, or through that of their clients, family members, employees, or domestic staff.

Any improvement or remodeling work carried out by the Tenants on the Property must be previously authorized in writing by the Landlord, and such improvements shall remain for the benefit of the Property without the Landlord being obligated to pay any amount.

Any violation of the terms agreed upon in this clause shall be the responsibility of the Tenants for any damages caused.

SEVENTH.- The Tenants shall bear all expenses corresponding to the internal maintenance of the Property, except those related to hidden defects, which shall be the responsibility of the Landlord.

Both parties likewise expressly agree that the Tenants are obligated to maintain the air conditioning units and refrigerator every five months, at their own expense, and such maintenance shall be performed by technicians assigned by the Landlord.

The Tenants shall also pay at their own expense for utility services such as: electricity, trash collection, gas, telephone, cable television, Internet, etc., with the parties agreeing that simple copies, after verification against the original receipts for payment of these services, shall be delivered by the Tenants to the Landlord, or a person designated for such purpose, who shall keep them to verify and monitor that the Tenants are not behind on the payment of any of these services.

EIGHTH.- SUBLETTING. The Tenants are prohibited from subletting, transferring, lending, or assigning in any form the use of the leased property or the rights under this contract. Any subleases, transfers, loans, or assignments made in violation of this clause shall be null and void and ineffective against the Landlord, and shall give rise to forced execution, which both parties agree shall include the immediate vacancy of the Property under the terms provided in the eleventh and twelfth clauses.

NINTH.- EARLY TERMINATION. The parties agree that the Landlord may terminate this Contract early and consequently evict the Tenants in the event that they fail to comply with any of the obligations contained in this Contract, and especially in any of the following circumstances:

a) If they fail to timely pay two or more months of Rent, pursuant to the second clause.
b) If they fail to timely pay two or more months of any utility service available at the Property, pursuant to the seventh clause.
c) If they change the use or purpose of the Property without written authorization from the Landlord, pursuant to the fifth clause.
d) If they make modifications or remodeling to the Property without written authorization from the Landlord, pursuant to the sixth clause.
e) If they sublet, assign, or transfer by any means the rights under this Contract without the Landlord's authorization, pursuant to the eighth clause.
f) If they introduce toxic or explosive substances into the Property.
g) In general, if they fail to comply with any of the obligations stipulated in this Contract.
h) Death of the Tenants, in addition to the provisions of the tenth clause.

TENTH.- In the event of the death of the Tenants, if there are family members, they shall have a period of 30 days to surrender "THE PROPERTY" paying each and every debt; having done so, they shall have the right to receive the deposit amount. In the event that there are no family members, both parties agree that the Landlord may open the apartment and place personal belongings under safekeeping for a period of 45 days, or sooner if a family member claims them; after that period, they may be sold or shall become the Landlord's possession.

In the event of the Landlord's death, the Tenants shall have 45 days to surrender "THE PROPERTY" to the family members or their legal representatives.

The parties also agree that if there is any debt owed by the Tenants at the time of said vacancy, it shall be settled at that time. Otherwise, the Landlord expresses her consent that in the case of eviction or enforcement proceedings as provided in the Code of Civil Procedures for the State of Quintana Roo, her property within or outside the leased property may be seized.

ELEVENTH.- SPECIAL CLAUSES. The Tenants are not authorized to bring pets into the property without the consent of the Landlord; if the Tenants violate this clause, the penalty shall be in accordance with the thirteenth clause of this contract.

TWELFTH.- If the Tenants do not comply with the term stipulated in the first clause of this contract, the return of the security deposit is nullified. If they do not have the money to comply with this clause, the Tenants agree that their furniture and personal articles of sufficient value may be seized to cover the amount.

THIRTEENTH.- CONTRACTUAL PENALTIES. The parties establish as a contractual penalty the payment of an amount equivalent to twice the Rent (the "Contractual Penalty"), which the Tenants shall pay in favor of the Landlord in the event that any of the circumstances listed in the ninth clause are triggered.

Furthermore, if the Landlord must pursue eviction proceedings due to the Contract having expired, the Tenants shall pay the Contractual Penalty for each month of occupancy after expiration, regardless of the Consideration that continues to accrue, without the Contract being considered renewed.

The obligation to pay a Contractual Penalty may arise for each of the aforementioned circumstances; that is, as many Contractual Penalties shall be paid as there are circumstances that are triggered at the Property.

Payment of the Contractual Penalty does not limit the Landlord's right to pursue early termination of the Contract.

FOURTEENTH.- For the purposes of receiving and hearing notifications and other matters, the parties designate the following addresses:

The Landlord: Avenida Álamos, Supermanzana 311, Paseo las Quintas, Manzana 26, Lote 1, Casa 4, in the City of Cancún, Benito Juárez, State of Quintana Roo.

The Tenants: THE PROPERTY that is the subject of this contract.

In the event that either party wishes to use a different address for such purposes, they must notify the other party in writing; otherwise, any notification, requirement, or summons made at the addresses cited herein shall have full legal force.

FIFTEENTH.- This contract cancels and renders null and void any prior agreement, whether oral or written, that the parties may have made in relation to this matter.

SIXTEENTH.- For all matters not provided for in this contract, the provisions of the Civil Code of the State of Quintana Roo shall apply, and the competent courts of the City of Cancún shall have jurisdiction over any controversy arising from, originating from, or derived from the execution, fulfillment, or interpretation of this Contract, with the parties expressly waiving any other jurisdiction that may correspond to them by reason of their present or future domicile.
