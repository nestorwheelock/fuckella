## Key Legal Arguments Summary

### 1. No Manifestation of Will (Art. 383(d)) — Nuclear Option
The tenant is a US national who does not speak Spanish. The contract was drafted entirely in Spanish with no translation provided. Under Art. 383(d), when a person does not know the language of the contract, **no manifestation of will exists.** This creates **absolute nullity** — imprescriptible, cannot be ratified, can be raised at any time.

### 2. Systematic Fraud / Dolo (Arts. 198, 203) — Multi-Year Pattern
The landlord made undisclosed modifications to the contract every year while telling the tenant "only the price changed" or "nothing changed." With no attorney present after Year 1 and a tenant who cannot read Spanish, she exploited the language barrier to insert unfavorable terms. This is textbook dolo (fraud) and reticencia (deliberate concealment).

### 3. Tacita Reconduccion (Art. 2739) — Automatic Indefinite Renewal
The tenant paid March 2026 rent at $12,000 on March 1. The landlord accepted without objection. When the contract expires March 11 and the tenant continues in possession, the lease automatically renews for an **indefinite term at $12,000/month.** No new contract needed.

### 4. Rent Increase Never Justified (Art. 2738)
Art. 2738 caps increases at 10% AND requires the landlord to prove with zone-specific market evidence that rents have risen. She never provided evidence for any increase. The very first increase (11.1%) already exceeded the cap.

### 5. Mandatory Maintenance Violations (Art. 2687)
The landlord's maintenance obligations are mandatory by law and cannot be waived by contract. The 3-month AC replacement delay in Caribbean tropical heat, failure to fulfill WhatsApp promises, and shifting repair costs to the tenant all violate Art. 2687.

### 6. Right of First Refusal (Art. 2708)
After 5+ years of tenancy with significant improvements, the tenant has the right of first refusal on both re-leasing and purchase of the property. The landlord cannot simply replace the tenant.
